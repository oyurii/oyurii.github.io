Course Description

This course provides a systematic introduction to non-relativistic quantum mechanics. The course covers the fundamental postulates of quantum theory, operator formalism, Schrödinger equation, quantum systems in one and three dimensions, atomic structure, spin, identical particles, approximation methods, and basic concepts of molecular quantum mechanics.

Lecture Topics and Questions

1. Introduction to Quantum Mechanics. Wave Function

  1. Subject and methods of quantum mechanics.
  2. Experimental and theoretical prerequisites for the development of quantum mechanics.
  3. Necessity of probabilistic and statistical interpretation of de Broglie waves.
  4. Wave function. Probability density. Normalization condition. General requirements for the wave function.

2. Elementary Theory of Linear Operators

  1. Definition of an operator. Operations with operators. Commutator, anticommutator. Kernel of an operator.
  2. Eigenfunctions and eigenvalues. Degeneracy.
  3. Linear, complex conjugate, inverse, transpose, Hermitian-conjugate and self-adjoint (Hermitian) operators.
  4. Properties of operators:
    • properties of eigenvalues of linear self-adjoint operators;
    • properties of eigenfunctions of linear self-adjoint operators;
    • superposition principle;
    • completeness of eigenfunction systems;
    • eigenfunctions of commuting operators;
    • simultaneous measurement of physical observables.

3. Operators of Physical Observables. Schrödinger Equation

  1. Operators of physical observables in quantum mechanics. Time-dependent Schrödinger equation.
  2. Self-adjointness of the Hamiltonian operator.
  3. Expectation values of physical observables.
  4. Time differentiation of operators. Constants of motion in quantum mechanics.

4. Space-Time Properties and Conservation Laws. Correspondence Principle

  1. Relation between conservation laws and symmetry invariance of the Hamiltonian.
  2. Conservation of momentum, angular momentum, and energy.
  3. Parity. Parity conservation law.
  4. Equations of motion in quantum mechanics. Ehrenfest theorem. Bohr correspondence principle.

5. Wave Packets. Heisenberg Uncertainty Relations

  1. Particle modeling using wave packets.
  2. Derivation of Heisenberg uncertainty relations using wave packets.
  3. Operator derivation of uncertainty relations.
  4. Experimental verification of uncertainty relations.

6. Stationary Schrödinger Equation. One-Dimensional Problems

  1. Stationary states and stationarity condition.
  2. Free particle.
  3. Normalization of wave functions with continuous spectrum.
  4. Particle in a one-dimensional infinite potential well.
  5. Probability current density. Tunneling effect.

7. Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

  1. Formulation of the quantum harmonic oscillator problem.
  2. Solution of the Schrödinger equation and analysis of results.

8. Quantum Theory of the Hydrogen Atom

  1. Quantum mechanical operators in spherical coordinates.
  2. Schrödinger equation in spherical coordinates. Radial and angular equations.

9. Solution of the Angular Schrödinger Equation

  1. Solution of the angular equation.
  2. Quantization of angular momentum and its projection. Orbital and magnetic quantum numbers.

10. Solution of the Radial Schrödinger Equation

  1. Solution of the radial equation.
  2. Energy quantization. Principal quantum number.

11. Spatial Structure of the Hydrogen Atom. Electron Spin

  1. Angular and radial probability distributions.
  2. Angular momentum and magnetic moment of the electron.
  3. Electron spin and its mathematical description.
  4. Quantum state of an electron including spin.

12. Quantum Mechanics of Many-Particle Systems

  1. Wave function and operators for many-particle systems.
  2. Principle of indistinguishability of identical particles.
  3. Symmetric and antisymmetric wave functions. Pauli exclusion principle.
  4. Systems of identical bosons and fermions.

13. Approximation Methods in Quantum Mechanics

  1. Overview of approximation methods.
  2. Time-independent perturbation theory (non-degenerate case).
  3. Perturbation theory with degeneracy.
  4. Ritz variational method.

14. Many-Electron Atoms. Periodic Table

  1. Valence electron model of alkali atoms.
  2. Helium atom in the first-order perturbation theory.
  3. Periodic table of elements. Ground-state electron configurations.
  4. Hydrogen molecule. Exchange interaction.

15. Angular Momentum of Many-Particle Systems

  1. Properties of angular momentum operators.
  2. LS coupling and jj coupling.
  3. Addition of angular momenta.
  4. Addition of electron spins.
  5. Orbital, spin, and total angular momentum. Quantum numbers L, S, J.

16. Vector Model of the Atom. Atomic Spectra

  1. Spectral multiplets. Spin-orbit interaction. Selection rules. Forbidden transitions.
  2. Atomic terms. Hund’s rules.
  3. Gyromagnetic ratio.
  4. Construction of the vector model of the atom.
  5. Zeeman effect.

17. Nature of Chemical Bonding

  1. Hydrogen molecule bond energy (Heitler–London method).
  2. Physical interpretation of chemical bonding forces.
  3. Van der Waals forces.
  4. Vibrational and rotational energy levels of the hydrogen molecule.
